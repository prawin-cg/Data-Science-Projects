Categorising Coutries¶

Objective:¶

To categorise the countries using socio-economic and health factors that determine the overall development of the country.

About organization:¶

HELP International is an international humanitarian NGO that is committed to fighting poverty and providing the people of backward countries with basic amenities and relief during the time of disasters and natural calamities.

HELP International have been able to raise around $ 10 million. Now the CEO of the NGO needs to decide how to use this money strategically and effectively. So, CEO has to make decision to choose the countries that are in the direst need of aid. Hence, your Job as a Data scientist is to categorise the countries using some socio-economic and health factors that determine the overall development of the country. Then you need to suggest the countries which the CEO needs to focus on the most.

About the Dataset:¶

country: Name of the country.
child_mort: Death of children under 5 years of age per 1000 births.
exports: Exports of goods and services per capita. Given as % of GDP per capita.
health: Total health spending per capita. Given as %age of GDP per capita
imports: Imports of goods and services per capita. Given as %age of the GDP per capita
Income: Net income per person
Inflation: The measurement of the annual growth rate of the Total GDP
life_expec: The average number of years a new born child would live if the current mortality patterns are to remain the same
total_fer: The number of children that would be born to each woman if the current age-fertility rates remain the same.
gdpp: The GDP per capita. Calculated as the Total GDP divided by the total population.

Source: https://www.kaggle.com/rohan0301/unsupervised-learning-on-country-data

Principal Component Analysis¶

It is a technique to reduce the curse of dimensionality. We can also use this to capture the variance explained by the different features of the dataset in the form of principal components. This could be helpful in redundance of variance explained by many features. As we have many stongly correlated features in our dataset, we will carry out this and use these components for further processes.

Before we do PCA on our dataset we will standardise the data using zscores by applying standard scaler.

Inferences:¶

By observing the centroids, we could make certain inferences about the cluster labels:

  • Cluster 2 has the highest child mortality rate. Thes countries in the cluster 2 must be taken care of the children being born. Cluster 1 also has considerable high amount of child mortality rate.
  • Cluster 0 has the highest export GDP percentage per capita and cluster 2 has the lowest.
  • Health wise expenditure percentage from GDP per capita has been more or less equal in the clusters 0,1,2 but highest in the cluster 3.
  • Imports GDP percentage per capita is highest in cluster 0 and more or less equal among all the other clusters.
  • Net income per person is the highest in the cluster 0 countries and lowest in the cluster 2 countries.
  • Inflation is being highest in the cluster 2 countries whereas the cluster 0 and cluster 3 have more or less same inflation average.
  • Life expectancy has been the highest in the cluster 0 and cluster 3 countries whereas cluster 2 is lowest in life expectancy.
  • Total fertility being the highest in cluster 2 and more or less in the same range in all other cluster countries.
  • GDP per capita is lowest in the cluster 2 countries and highest in the cluster 0 countries.

Key Recommendations:¶

  • Cluster 2 has been very affected in terms of health and economy. It has the lowest GDP per capita, net income per person, high child mortality, low exports GDP percentage, low life expectancy. Mainly concerned countries come under cluster 2 and hence immediate necessary actions to be taken starting from these countries.
  • Cluster 1 countries are the next immediate group of countries that needs to be taken care of. These are the countries have second lowest GDP, net income per person, life expectancy.
  • If we look at cluster 0 countries, improving the percentage of exports and imports could be one of the key things that could uplift the countries both economically and health wise beacause the well doing countries in the cluster 0 have a huge difference in the imports and exports percentage of GDP per capita.
  • And also there should be more spending on health on cluster 2 countries like cluster 3 countries from the GDP as the cluster 2 countries lack some health facilities which reflects heavily in child mortality and life expectancy.
